Beneath the surface, roots cross without regard for ownership. Willow threads intertwine with alder, spreading laterally where depth offers little room. Mud fills the spaces between them, held in place by what cannot be seen from above.

When the water rises again, this work will matter. The creek depends on these quiet structures, built slowly and without display. Nothing here stands alone long enough to fail alone. Each connection permits movement above while preventing disruption below.

Below the visible, alliances form. Roots cross, press, yield, and brace. No single root holds the bank. Strength here is distributed, quiet, unclaimed. When one weakens, others compensate without announcement. The creek teaches that resilience is not heroism but relationship maintained under pressure.

Support, in this place, is shared.
Stability is the product of many small holdings.
